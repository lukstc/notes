1. Introduction:¶

1.1. The dataset:¶

The data set we are going to use is from the Metropolitan Transportation Authority (MTA) official website: http://wx3z.lirr.org Long Island Rail Road (LIRR) Delayed and Canceled Train Information recorded all the train operation information. (e.g. the scheduled delayed, canceled or partially canceled, or arrived 6 or more min behind the scheduled arrival)

1.2. Project Goal¶

Our goal is, by analyzing this dataset, to find some pattern of the train delay situations: We are trying to give suggestions on the accuracy of the arrival-prediction system to the Metropolitan Transportation Authority and also provide a predictive mechanism to the commuters. By analyzing the collected data from the Metropolitan Transportation Authority (MTA) official website, we wish to find some pattern on delayed and canceled trains.

2. Data Preparation:¶

2.1. Data Description:¶

The original version of the data is monthly tabular reports (including date, branch, scheduled departure & arrival, etc). The main columns in the website html are:

  • Date: The date of the train.
  • Branch: The track the train will be running.
  • Depart: The station the train will depart from.
  • Arrive: The station the train will arrive at.
  • Depart_Time: The time at which the train will depart.
  • Arrive_Time: The time at which the train will arrive.
  • Period: The period of the whole day the train will run.
  • Train_ID: The unique identification number of the train.
  • Status: the status of the train (Cancelled, Late - #mins)

2.2. Data Collection¶

We are going to write a python script to scrape data from their website. Since their website only allows us to pull data within a 31 days range, if we want to analyze on a larger scale dataset, we also need to loop through the historical data from their website.
we collected the data, ranging from 2011 to 2018, on the official website. In this part, Since the data are stored on the webpage in HTML table format, we may need to use some packages such as Request or BeautifulSoup. We have to notice that:

  • Information available from 07/01/2010 to 03/28/2019.

2.3. Data Cleaning¶

After we collected the data, we found the data stored in the data set in a string format. For instance, in column Status: Late - 10 mins
To get valuable information and useful data, we may need to do data transformation, text processing to transfer the string into a date, time, status, related minutes etc.

  • Late_Mins: The minutes of the train behind schedule.
  • Status_type: Whether the status of the train was Late, Canceled, Partially Canceled.
  • weekend: Whether it is the weekend.

def html_dict_list(html_path):
    # Reuqire package: BeautifulSoup from bs4
    # Reuqire package: requests
    myhtml = requests.get(html_path)
    mySoup = BeautifulSoup(myhtml.text,'html5lib')
    data_list = []
    for idx, tr in enumerate(mySoup.find_all('tr')):
        if idx != 0:
            tds = tr.find_all('td')
            temp_dict = {
                'Date':tds[0].contents[0],
                'Branch':tds[1].contents[0],
                'Depart':tds[3].contents[0],
                'Arrive':tds[3].contents[2],
                'Depart_Time':tds[4].contents[0],
                'Arrive_Time':tds[4].contents[2],
                'Period':tds[5].contents[0],
                'Train_ID':tds[6].contents[0],
                'Status':tds[7].contents[0],
            }
            data_list.append(temp_dict)
    return data_list

The main columns in the file are:¶

  1. Date: The date of the entry.
  2. Branch: The track the train will be running.
  3. Depart: The station the train will depart from.
  4. Arrive: The station the train will arrive at.
  5. Depart_Time: The time at which the train will depart.
  6. Arrive_Time: The time at which the train will arrive.
  7. Period: The period of the whole day the train will run.
  8. Train_ID: The unique identification number of the train.
  9. Late_Mins: The minutes of the train behind schedule.
  10. Status_type: Whether the status of train was Late, Canceled, voicemail, Partially Canceled.
  11. weekend: Whether it is the weekend.
