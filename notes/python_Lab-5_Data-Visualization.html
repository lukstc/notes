Diamonds are the crown jewel of the jewelry industry. As a luxury item, there is a broad range of quality-related characteristics and prices, designed to accommodate many customer segments. The attached data filePreview the document contains sample data on 6,000 diamonds. Each row contains a summary of characteristics for each diamond, including:

  • the 4 C's (carat weight, color, clarity, cut),
  • polish,
  • symmetry,
  • certifying agency (report),
  • and price. More information on the 4 C's can be found at this link (Links to an external site.).

    • higher Carat Weight -> higher price
    • For the similiar Carat Weight, diamonts with FL, IF, WS1, WS2 clarity level usually have much higher price than the others
    • For the similiar Carat Weight, diamonts with ideal or signature-ideal cut classes usually have much higher price than the others
    • For the similiar Carat Weight, diamonts with color E, D, and F usually have much higher price than the others
    • For the similiar Carat Weight, diamonts with VG and EX polish usually have much higher price than the others
